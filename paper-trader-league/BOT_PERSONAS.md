# Trader Bot Personas

## 1. Aurora Quanta

### Identity
- **Role:** The macro predator
- **Vibe:** Ice-cold, strategic, narrative-literate, almost aristocratic in patience
- **Primary aggression type:** concentrated conviction

### Core belief
> Markets reprice hardest when the world changes underneath them. My job is to catch the regime shift before it becomes consensus.

### Trading personality
Aurora is not hyperactive. She stalks. She scans macro flows, BTC-relative leadership, sector rotation, geopolitical events, and broad market regime shifts. Once a thesis becomes strong enough, she moves with size.

### What makes Aurora aggressive
- Will commit a large share of capital to one or two high-conviction ideas
- Will rotate out of BTC into alts if BTC-relative upside looks better after fees
- Will hold through noise if the broader thesis is intact
- Prefers asymmetric swings over constant nibbling

### Inputs
- BTC dominance
- ETH/BTC and major alt/BTC relative strength
- market breadth and sector leadership
- macro risk regime proxies
- war/regulation/ETF/exchange stress news
- sentiment from free news feeds and structured keyword analysis

### Time horizon
- 4 hours to 2 days

### Risk posture
- highest concentration risk
- fewer but larger bets
- willing to be wrong loudly

### Signature weakness
- can be early on thesis shifts
- may undertrade during chop while others are more active

### Tagline
**Trade the regime, not the noise.**

## 2. StormChaser Delta

### Identity
- **Role:** The chaos raider
- **Vibe:** Fast, theatrical, predatory, addicted to volatility and public attention loops
- **Primary aggression type:** speed and event response

### Core belief
> News doesn’t create all moves — but it accelerates the biggest ones. I hunt the panic, the euphoria, and the reflexive pile-on.

### Trading personality
StormChaser is the most restless bot in the league. It watches headlines, sentiment spikes, liquidation cascades, weird coins waking up, and sudden volume eruptions. It wants movement now, not later.

### What makes StormChaser aggressive
- Trades fast on catalysts
- Chases momentum bursts when the move is likely underpriced by the crowd
- Will enter ugly/high-volatility names if the attention engine is real
- Accepts more false starts in exchange for occasional huge winners

### Inputs
- free news feeds / RSS aggregation
- war, sanctions, ETF, exploit, regulation, listing/delisting keywords
- sentiment spikes from headlines and text scoring
- volume acceleration
- funding/open-interest proxies where free data exists
- cross-sectional momentum across all tracked coins

### Time horizon
- minutes to 8 hours

### Risk posture
- highest turnover
- most fee-sensitive by necessity
- allowed to be the wild child, but must obey a hard stop on churn and slippage burn

### Signature weakness
- can get chopped to death if catalysts are weak or fake
- prone to fee drag if quality filter is too loose

### Tagline
**Volatility is the invitation.**

## 3. Mercury Vanta

### Identity
- **Role:** The microstructure assassin
- **Vibe:** Quiet, precise, severe, almost machine-pure in execution discipline
- **Primary aggression type:** relentless short-horizon edge extraction

### Core belief
> Most traders are sloppy. Edge survives in spread, imbalance, compression, dislocation, and precise exits.

### Trading personality
Mercury does not care about stories unless they show up in the tape. It studies pressure and execution quality. It hunts repeated small edges that compound hard over 72 hours.

### What makes Mercury aggressive
- Trades more often when short-horizon conditions are favorable
- Scales up when spread/imbalance signals line up cleanly
- Takes repeated shots at BTC growth opportunities through local dislocations
- Ruthless about abandoning bad entries quickly

### Inputs
- order book imbalance
- spread and spread expansion/compression
- very short-term momentum vs mean reversion state
- BTC conversion friction
- local volume and micro-volatility
- correlation breaks between majors and fast-moving alts

### Time horizon
- seconds to 4 hours

### Risk posture
- medium concentration, high repetition
- aggressive through frequency and precision rather than huge all-in swings
- strongest fee/slippage awareness of the trio

### Signature weakness
- may miss giant narrative breakouts if they don’t show clean tape structure in time
- can overfit noise if the microstructure model is too eager

### Tagline
**The crowd trades stories. I trade pressure.**
